Lay's 

Lay's story goes back all the way to 1932. Herman Lay, a salesman, purchased a food manufacturer in Atlanta, Georgia. From there on, it is unclear as to how exactly the potato chip was developed. But as a salesman would, Herman Lay went door to door and sold his product. Decades later, Mr. Lay decided to parter with Charles Doolin. The owner of the Frito Company, another food manufacturer during the 1960s. The merging of both businesses led to what is now known as the Frito-Lay Company.Funnily enough, Frito-Lay eventually merged with the Pepsi-Cola company in 1965. Pepsi-Cola, now known as PepsiCo is a soft-drink giant we are all familiar with. Frito-Lay's contribution to the partnership is directly within the snack foods industry.
